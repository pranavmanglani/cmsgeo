Geography – Earthquake

Understanding tectonic forces and seismic activity

EARTHQUAKE
Stress builds at plate boundaries until rocks fracture along faults.
Energy is released suddenly and travels as seismic waves.
The focus is the point underground where the quake begins.
The epicenter lies directly above the focus on the surface.

Theory

Earthquakes occur due to the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust caused by tectonic stress. This stress builds up over time as tectonic plates move slowly.

The point where the earthquake originates beneath the surface is called the focus. The epicenter is the point on the surface directly above it.

When rocks break along faults, seismic waves are produced. These waves travel through the Earth and cause ground shaking.

Earthquakes are most common along plate boundaries such as convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries.

Understanding earthquakes helps engineers design structures that can resist seismic damage.
