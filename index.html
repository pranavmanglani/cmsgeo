Introduction

Earthquakes are among the most destructive natural disasters on Earth. They occur due to sudden movements within the Earth’s crust. This project aims to understand earthquakes not only as geological events, but also as phenomena that deeply affect human society and development.

Week 1 – Case Studies & Human Impact

In the first week, we studied earthquakes in Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey. We observed that regions with similar earthquake magnitudes experienced different levels of destruction.

This led us to understand that human preparedness, building standards, economic strength, and emergency response play a crucial role in reducing loss of life and property.

Week 2 – Plate Tectonics

The Earth’s crust is divided into tectonic plates that float over the mantle. These plates are constantly moving, though very slowly.

Earthquakes occur mainly along plate boundaries:

  • Convergent boundaries – plates collide
  • Divergent boundaries – plates move apart
  • Transform boundaries – plates slide past each other

Week 3 – Seismology & Earthquake Measurement

Seismology is the scientific study of earthquakes. Earthquakes produce seismic waves that travel through the Earth.

  • P-waves – fastest, travel through solids and liquids
  • S-waves – slower, travel only through solids

The focus (hypocenter) is the point inside the Earth where the earthquake originates, while the epicenter is the point directly above it on the surface.

Week 4 – Earthquake Risk in India

India is divided into seismic zones based on earthquake risk. Zone V is the most active, while Zone II is the least active.

Understanding local seismic zones helps in designing safer buildings and improving disaster preparedness.

Safety Measures

  1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On during shaking
  2. Stay away from windows and heavy furniture
  3. Keep an emergency kit ready
