The Global Seismic Venture

An Experimental Learning Project that explores earthquakes through scientific understanding, geographical analysis, and human impact.

Presented by: Shrijal, Ajitendra, Vaidik & Inisha

Week 1 – First Insights (Shrijal)

In the first week, we built an earthquake model and studied case examples from Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey. This activity helped us understand how seismic energy causes destruction.

We learned that earthquake damage is influenced not only by magnitude, but also by building quality, preparedness, and socio-economic conditions.

Week 2 – Plate Tectonics (Ajitendra)

We studied convergent, divergent, and transform plate boundaries. Earthquakes occur due to the continuous movement of tectonic plates and the sudden release of built-up stress.

This week helped us connect earthquake distribution with Earth’s internal geological processes.

Week 3 – Seismology & Analysis (Vaidik)

We learned about seismic waves such as P-waves and S-waves and how scientists use seismographs to analyze earthquakes.

Understanding the focus (hypocenter) and epicenter helped us see how data, mathematics, and physics are essential in geography.

Week 4 – India’s Seismic Risk (Inisha)

We studied India’s seismic zones and understood that different regions face different levels of earthquake risk.

Preparedness, earthquake-resistant construction, and awareness are essential for reducing damage.
