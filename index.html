The Global Seismic Venture

A multi-disciplinary exploration into the geographic, scientific, and human consequences of tectonic activity.

Week 1: The Human Geography of Risk

Our journey began by comparing major earthquakes in Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey. We learned that the "Why" of a disaster is often linked to human factors rather than just the magnitude of the tremor[cite: 7].

  • Vulnerability: Socio-economic status determines survival[cite: 7].
  • Preparedness: Strict building codes are the primary defense against ground displacement[cite: 7].
Figure 1: Building code effectiveness in earthquake zones [cite: 7]

Week 2: Tectonic Mechanics

We tracked real-time tremors to understand that seismic activity is a predictable consequence of the movement of the Earth’s lithosphere[cite: 11]. Plates are in constant, slow motion over the asthenosphere, creating immense stress at their boundaries[cite: 11].

Week 3: The Science of Seismology

This phase focused on interpreting the data. We studied how P-waves (Primary) and S-waves (Secondary) travel through the Earth. Locating the epicenter and hypocenter allows scientists to calculate the exact energy release[cite: 15].

Essential Safety & Preparedness [cite: 20, 23]

DROP, COVER, HOLD ON

Get low, seek cover under sturdy furniture, and hold firm[cite: 25].

AVOID HAZARDS

Stay away from glass, windows, and unsecured tall furniture[cite: 26].
