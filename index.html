The Mechanics of Earthquakes

Our project explores the profound geographic and human consequences of tectonic activity. By understanding the relationship between the Focus and the Epicenter, we can better predict structural outcomes.

  • Focus: The point deep inside the Earth where the rupture begins.
  • Epicenter: The point on the surface directly above the focus.

Week 1: Socio-Economic Vulnerability

Shrijal: "Our key learning from case studies—Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey—was that vulnerability is not just geological, but socio-economic. The same magnitude of quake results in vastly different human impacts based on building codes, preparedness, and economic resources."

Week 2 & 3: Tectonic Dynamics

Ajitendra & Vaidik: "Tracking tremors allows us to understand the 'Why' of earthquakes. Seismic activity is a predictable consequence of plate movement: Convergent, Divergent, and Transform."

Energy travels via P-Waves (Primary) and S-Waves (Secondary). Using Seismographs, we calculate the magnitude on the Richter Scale to quantify the disaster.

Week 4: Localized Risk in India

Inisha: "Our final week focused on applying knowledge to India. We plotted India's seismic zones (V, IV, III, II) to understand the degrees of threat across our nation. Knowing your local zone is the first step toward mitigation."

Essential Safety Protocols

Safety First: Mitigation starts with public awareness and personal preparedness.

  1. DROP, COVER, HOLD ON: Immediate indoor protocol.
  2. AVOID HAZARDS: Stay away from glass and unsecured furniture.
  3. MAINTAIN YOUR KIT: 72 hours of water, food, and first-aid.
