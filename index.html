Week 1: Socio-Economic Insights

The Geography of Risk

Pranav & Medhansh: "Our analysis of case studies—Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey—revealed that vulnerability is socio-economic. While seismic energy is geological, the human toll is dictated by building codes, preparedness levels, and national wealth."

Developing regions often face catastrophic loss from the same magnitude of quake that a well-prepared nation might survive with minimal damage.

Week 2 & 3: Tectonic Mechanics

Plate Boundaries & Wave Physics

Ansh & Aditya: "Seismic activity is a predictable consequence of the slow movement of tectonic plates at Convergent, Divergent, and Transform boundaries."

We analyze energy using instrumentation:

  • P-Waves (Primary): Longitudinal waves that arrive first.
  • S-Waves (Secondary): Transverse waves that cause the most shaking and destruction.

Week 4: Localized Hazard

India's Seismic Zonation (BIS)

Piyush: "Over 60% of India is prone to moderate to severe shaking. We mapped India into seismic zones (II through V) based on the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)."

Zone V represents areas like the Himalayas and the Rann of Kutch—areas of Highest Damage Risk. Knowing your zone is the first step in disaster mitigation.

Final Takeaway

Safety & Emergency Preparedness

Mitigation is a shared responsibility. We conclude our project with three essential life-saving protocols:

  • DROP, COVER, HOLD ON: Immediate indoor response.
  • AVOID HAZARDS: Stay away from glass, windows, and elevators.
  • EMERGENCY KIT: 72 hours of water, food, and medical supplies.
