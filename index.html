The Global Seismic Venture

A multi-disciplinary exploration into the geographic, scientific, and human consequences of tectonic activity.

Week 1: Socio-Economic Vulnerability

We studied Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey to understand that disaster impact is not just about magnitude. Vulnerability is tied to building codes and economic resources.

Key Findings:

  • Building codes are the primary defense against collapse.
  • Symmetrical plans and low height-to-base ratios improve safety.

Week 2: Tectonic Mechanics

Seismic activity is a predictable consequence of plate movements. We explored how plates interact at their boundaries to release massive energy.

Week 3: Seismic Waves

Seismology relies on tracking P-waves and S-waves. P-waves are faster and travel through liquids, while S-waves are slower and only travel through solids.

Figure 2: P-waves arrive first (Primary); S-waves follow and cause more surface shaking.

Week 4: India's New Hazard Map

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) updated the map in 2025, introducing Zone VI for the highest risk areas like the entire Himalayan arc.

  • 61% of India's landmass is now in moderate-to-high risk zones.
  • Zone VI covers the Himalayas from J&K to Arunachal Pradesh.
