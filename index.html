The Global Seismic Venture

An Experimental Learning Project exploring earthquakes through geography, science, data analysis, and human preparedness.

Team: Shrijal • Ajitendra • Vaidik • Inisha

Introduction

Earthquakes are sudden vibrations of the Earth’s crust caused by the release of stored energy along tectonic plate boundaries. This project studies earthquakes as both natural phenomena and human disasters.

Week 1 – Case Studies & Human Geography

Our analysis of earthquakes in Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey showed that the same magnitude earthquake can have very different impacts.

✔ Strong building codes reduce damage
✔ Emergency response saves lives
✔ Poverty increases vulnerability

Week 2 – Plate Tectonics

The Earth’s lithosphere is divided into tectonic plates that move slowly over the asthenosphere. Stress builds up at plate boundaries.

Week 3 – Seismology & Measurement

Seismology uses seismic waves to measure earthquakes. P-waves travel fastest, while S-waves are slower but more destructive.

Week 4 – Earthquake Risk in India

India is divided into seismic zones ranging from Zone II to Zone V. Zone V areas such as the Himalayas are most earthquake-prone.
