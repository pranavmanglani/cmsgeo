The Global Seismic Venture

An Experimental Learning Project exploring earthquakes through science, geography, and human impact.

Team: Shrijal • Ajitendra • Vaidik • Inisha

Introduction

Earthquakes occur due to sudden movements within the Earth’s crust. They release enormous energy that travels as seismic waves, causing the ground to shake and structures to collapse.

Week 1 – Case Studies & Human Impact

Our study of Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey showed that the same magnitude earthquake can cause very different damage depending on preparedness, construction quality, and economic conditions.

Key Observation
Prepared regions → Less damage
Unprepared regions → Severe loss

Week 2 – Plate Tectonics

The Earth’s crust is broken into tectonic plates that move slowly. Earthquakes mainly occur at convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries.
