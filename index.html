ELP - Experimental Learning Project

Definition:
An Experimental Learning Project is a learning approach where students gain knowledge, skills, and experience by actively engaging in real-world tasks or experiments rather than just theoretical study. It emphasizes learning by doing, reflecting, and applying concepts practically.

Key Points:

  • Hands-on Experience: Learners actively participate in activities, experiments, or projects.
  • Practical Application: Focuses on applying theoretical knowledge to real-life situations.
  • Observation and Experimentation: Students observe outcomes, test ideas, and draw conclusions.
  • Problem-Solving: Encourages critical thinking and finding solutions to real challenges.
  • Reflection: Learners analyze results and experiences to improve understanding.
  • Skill Development: Enhances technical, analytical, and interpersonal skills.
  • Engagement: Promotes active learning and motivation compared to passive learning.
