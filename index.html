Week 1: Socio-Economic Vulnerability

In Week One, we conducted a case study analysis of four major seismic events: Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey. Our core finding was the "Vulnerability Gap."

We learned that seismic risk is not just about the magnitude of the earthquake. The human impact—death tolls and economic loss—is dictated by socio-economic factors, building codes, and preparedness levels.

Seismic Waves

Energy travels via P-Waves (Primary), which are the fastest and longitudinal, and S-Waves (Secondary), which arrive later and cause more damage. Instrumentation like seismographs allow us to measure these and calculate the Richter Scale magnitude.

Week 4: India's Hazard Profile

Our final week focused on local risk. India is mapped into different seismic zones by the BIS. Zone V represents the most dangerous areas.

By understanding our local zone, we can prioritize building standards and mitigation strategies.

Essential Safety Precautions

1. Drop, Cover, Hold On

Get low, get under sturdy cover, and hold firm until the shaking stops. This is the most effective indoor action.

2. Avoid Hazards

Stay clear of glass, windows, and unsecured furniture. If outdoors, move to an open area away from power lines.

3. Emergency Kit

Maintain a kit with water, non-perishable food, and a first-aid kit in an accessible location.
