Week 1: Socio-Economic Impact

Our project began by analyzing major earthquakes in Italy, Nepal, Gujarat, and Turkey. We discovered that the severity of an earthquake's impact is not just geological, but socio-economic.

Developing nations often face higher casualty rates due to a lack of enforced building codes and limited emergency resources. This realization shifted our focus toward the human geography of risk.

Week 2 & 3: Tectonic Understanding

Earthquakes are predictable consequences of plate movements—specifically at convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries. Using seismographs, we can measure the waves produced:

  • P-Waves: Primary waves that travel fastest and arrive first.
  • S-Waves: Secondary waves that move slower and cause significant ground shaking.

Week 4: India's Seismic Zonation

India is highly vulnerable, with over 60% of its land area prone to shaking. We categorized these into zones (II to V) based on the Bureau of Indian Standards.

Zone V (Red) includes the Himalayas and Rann of Kutch—areas of highest damage risk.

Safety & Preparedness

Preparedness is the key to survival. Inisha’s research highlights these critical actions:

1. Drop, Cover, Hold On

The standard protocol for indoor shaking. Protect your head and neck.

2. Evacuate Safely

Avoid elevators. Move to open spaces away from buildings and power lines.

3. Emergency Kit

Always keep a supply of water, non-perishable food, and first-aid ready.
